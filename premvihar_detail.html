spread over 73,000 square feet, Prem Vihar offers free education, with hostel accommodation for children, along with boarding and lodging facilities for destitute women. It features a fully equipped medical bay, resource center & occupational therapy rooms that also support children with intellectual disabilities, in addition to vocational training rooms, a spacious cafeteria and an entertainment room for the people housed there.In August 1924, a Women's Home of Service was established by the Women's Indian Association under the leadership of Mrs. Margaret Cousins and its Honorary General Secretary Mrs. Andal Venkatasubba Rao. It’s noble purpose was to provide refuge and training for distressed Indian women facing challenges such as widowhood, poverty, and desertion.

Younger girls’ study at the Madras Seva Sadan Higher Secondary School, while older girls and women receive counselling, training, and employment opportunities through other units within the organization preparing them to be emotionally stronger and economically independent, when they start their lives’ beyond the Sadan's doors.

One aspect that fills us with immense pride is that some of the NGOs in our coworking space, have been setup by our very own alumni from various MSS institutions.

The Madras Seva Sadan's reach has expanded to shelter and nurture numerous fledgling NGOs and non-profit institutions. Prem Vihar provides co-working spaces for NGOs and self-help groups who lack the means to acquire their own spaces. The Co-working space serves as a place for these NGOs to conduct programs, workshops, training sessions, and much more—completely free of charge!
