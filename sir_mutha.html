Sir. Mutha Venkatasubba Rao

Sir Mutha Venkatasubba Rao, a man of independent thought and fervent dedication to social reform, burst on the scene during the early 20th century, as a lawyer and then a Judge and Chief Justice for a record of 17 fruitful years. His contemporaries said of him that “his advocacy was forceful, dignified and fair”. His unwavering integrity and graceful demeanour earned him respect, titles, and honour – from friend and foe alike. 

 Sir. Mutha as lawyer

Portrait of Sir Mutha Career as a judge

 Delimitation committee with highlight on Sir Mutha Venkatasubba Rao

 Beyond his brilliant mind and clear thinking, it was his deep compassion for his people that endeared him to all, regardless of their stance. Knighted by the crown in 1936, and honoured with various medals for his social reform efforts, including the prestigious King George the Fifth Medal, he continued his stellar career after retirement from the bench, as the Agent of Berar, a region of dominion under the famous Nizam of Hyderabad.

 Sir Mutha the judge

Portrait with medals, as agent of berar

 The Nizam of Hyderabad’s Birthday Parade

  The Nizam of Hyderabad’s Birthday Parade

A trailblazer always, Sir Mutha Venketasubba Rao was a man of many “Firsts”! He was the youngest Judge to be elevated to the high court, and the “first” judge from the Original side. He was one of the early supporters and among the “first” scout commissioners’ in India. His generous patronage resulted in the building of the first swimming bath at Wenlock park for the early scout movement. He was also one of the few people who had the privilege of meeting Lord Baden Powell and was honoured by the Governor of Madras – Sir George Stanley, by unveiling his portrait in recognition of his distinguished service to the movement at the local Scouts Headquarters. This was in addition to the Seven Foot tall Portrait, painted by the famous C Nageswara Rao of Mumbai that was unveiled by Sir Lionel Leech, the then Chief Justice of the Madras High Court. In Sir Lionel’s own words – “I am sure that this portrait, which I am about to unveil, will be a source of inspiration to those who now and those who in future will walk in these corridors”.
